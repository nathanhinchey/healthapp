Prenatal Genetic Testing Education Guide

Intro Video

Welcome to your Prenatal Genetic Testing Education Guide. With the rapid advancement of medical and genetic technology, it can be hard to keep track of what prenatal genetic testing is available and what options are best for you. (Shots of science-y things. Tubes/pipetting.) This guide is designed to help you learn about your prenatal genetic testing options and identify the best strategy for you and your family. It's important to remember that genetic testing isn't right for everyone. There are many good reasons to undergo genetic testing during pregnancy, but whether or not you decide to undergo testing, it is always a personal choice based on what you feel is best for you and your family. (Shots of babies and families)


A few things to keep in mind as we begin to discuss prenatal genetic testing:

  • Everyone has about a 3 - 4% chance of having a child with a genetic condition or major birth defect. (Pie chart?)
  • All women have the right to prenatal testing even if that testing is invasive.(Picture of amnio?)
  • Although risk of certain genetic abnormalities increases with a mother's age, there are many genetic conditions that occur regardless of age. (Picture of young couple)
  • Certain things may increase your chances of having a child with a genetic condition. These include age and having a family member with a genetic condition. To assess your chances of having a child with a genetic condition, you can make an appointment with a genetic counselor.
  • (Screening vs. diagnostic?)
If you would like to learn a little about basic genetics and how these conditions can arise, you can continue on to our next video. You can also navigate through your testing options using the menu to the right.
